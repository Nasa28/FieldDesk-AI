# Hydraulic Pump 7000 — Troubleshooting

Service guide for the FieldDesk reference hydraulic pump. Use this when a
customer reports the pump is running but the equipment it drives is slow,
weak, or not moving at all.

## Symptoms

### Pressure Loss

Pressure loss at the gauge is the most common complaint. Confirm the gauge
itself is accurate before assuming the pump is at fault — gauges drift,
especially after impact damage. Swap with a known-good gauge and re-read.

Common causes, in order of likelihood:

- Worn inlet seal letting air into the suction line. Look for foaming in the
  reservoir sight glass.
- Clogged inlet filter or strainer. The 7000 uses a 40-micron screen on the
  suction side; replace if discoloured or torn.
- Bypass relief valve set too low. Factory spec is 2,500 PSI; field
  adjustment to 2,200 PSI is common on older units to reduce hose stress.
- Internal vane wear. After ~4,000 operating hours, output drops 8-12%.

### Slow Lift or Weak Cylinder Action

If pressure reads normal but the cylinder is slow, the issue is downstream:
restricted hoses, a sticky directional valve, or contaminated fluid.

- Drain a sample of hydraulic fluid into a clear container. Cloudy = water
  ingress. Particulate = wear debris. Both require a full flush.
- Inspect hoses for kinks, especially near the bend at the boom pivot.
- Cycle the directional valve manually with the engine off; sticky operation
  indicates contaminated spool bore.

## Parts to Bring

For a first-visit troubleshooting call, carry:

- 40-micron inlet strainer (P/N HP-7K-INLET-40)
- Inlet seal kit (P/N HP-7K-SEAL-INLET)
- 1 gallon ISO VG46 hydraulic fluid
- Spare 2,500 PSI relief cartridge
- Pressure gauge (0-3,500 PSI)

## Safety

Hydraulic systems store significant energy even with the engine off.

- Bleed all accumulators before disconnecting any line.
- Never trace a leak with your hand. Pinhole leaks at 2,500 PSI can inject
  hydraulic fluid through skin and require immediate ER attention.
- Wear safety glasses; ATF and hydraulic fluid splash injuries to the eye
  are common.
- Lock out and tag out the equipment per the customer's site procedures.

## Warranty Notes

The 7000 carries a 24-month manufacturer warranty on the pump body and
12 months on hoses. Inlet seals and strainers are wear items and are not
covered. Refer warranty disputes to dispatch with the unit serial number.

# Tankless Water Heater — Service Manual

Covers FieldDesk-supported tankless models from major manufacturers
(Rinnai, Navien, Rheem). Use this for "no hot water" calls and for
intermittent supply complaints.

## No Hot Water at Any Faucet

When the customer reports no hot water at any faucet in the house, the
issue is almost always upstream of the plumbing — either the unit isn't
firing or it's firing but the gas/electricity supply is interrupted.

### Step 1: Confirm the unit is powered

- Check the indicator light on the front panel. Solid green = idle and
  ready; flashing = error code (record the code, look up in the
  manufacturer manual you carry).
- For electric models, verify the 240V circuit breaker is closed.

### Step 2: Verify gas supply

- Open the gas shutoff valve at the unit and confirm gas pressure with a
  manometer if available. Static pressure should be 7-11" WC for natural
  gas, 11-13" WC for LP.
- Test another gas appliance (stove, dryer) in the home — if it's also out,
  the issue is the utility supply, not the heater.

### Step 3: Check the venting

Tankless units shut down on vent blockage to prevent CO buildup. Inspect
the intake and exhaust terminations outside the home for nests, debris, or
ice in cold-weather installations.

## Intermittent Hot Water

If the customer gets hot water at some faucets but not others, suspect a
crossover at a mixing valve or a failing aerator on a single fixture.

If hot water starts cold and warms over a long delay, the unit is firing
but the activation threshold is too high — flow rate at the affected
fixture is below the heater's minimum activation GPM (typically 0.5 GPM
for residential units). Clean fixture aerators and check pressure-reducing
valves.

## Parts to Bring

- Replacement flame rod (P/N RNI-FR-2)
- Combustion blower assembly (P/N varies — confirm model on the truck call)
- Igniter electrode + gasket kit
- Manometer for gas pressure verification
- Pump descaling kit and 1 gallon vinegar (annual descale)

## Safety

- Always test for CO before working on a firing unit. Tankless heaters can
  produce CO during partial blockage even when they otherwise appear to
  function normally.
- Shut off gas at the unit valve AND verify with a manometer that pressure
  has dropped before opening any gas-side fitting.
- Hot-side water lines can exceed 140°F at the unit outlet. Wear gloves.

## Warranty

Heat exchangers carry 12-15 year manufacturer warranties on residential
units, 5-10 years on commercial. Labor is NOT typically covered. The
customer must register the unit within 30 days of installation for the
warranty to be honored — many customers miss this. Confirm the install
date and registration before quoting any repair.

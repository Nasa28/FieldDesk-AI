# Lead Tech Warranty Policy

How to answer customer warranty questions in the field. When in doubt,
quote nothing — refer to dispatch and let the office issue the quote in
writing.

## What FieldDesk Covers

FieldDesk's standard warranty on labor, on work we performed:

- **90 days** on diagnostic work and parts replacement labor.
- **1 year** on full installations (new water heater, new HVAC unit, new
  electrical panel).
- **Lifetime on workmanship defects** — if a joint we soldered fails because
  the solder was bad, we cover the rework regardless of date. This does
  not cover wear-out of the parts themselves.

Customer-supplied parts are NOT covered under FieldDesk labor warranty
unless the customer can produce a receipt showing they purchased through a
FieldDesk-approved supplier (Home Depot Pro, Ferguson, Grainger). If the
customer bought the part on Amazon or eBay, no labor coverage.

## What FieldDesk Does NOT Cover

- Damage from misuse (running a tankless heater on natural gas after we
  installed it for propane, etc.).
- Damage from acts of nature (lightning, flood, freezing).
- Customer-installed parts.
- Cosmetic damage discovered after work completion.
- Consequential damage (e.g., water damage to flooring from a leak that
  the customer didn't report promptly).

## Manufacturer Warranties Pass Through

When we install a manufacturer-warranted product, the manufacturer's
warranty applies to the product, and our labor warranty applies to the
installation. These are separate. The customer may have to file directly
with the manufacturer for product defects.

Common manufacturer warranty lengths:

- Rinnai tankless: 12-15 years heat exchanger, 5 years parts, 1 year labor.
- Rheem residential HVAC: 10 years parts (with registration), 5 years
  unregistered.
- Lennox: 10 years parts, lifetime compressor on select models.
- Customer must REGISTER the product within 30-60 days for the long
  warranty. If they didn't register, the warranty drops to the
  unregistered tier (usually 5 years).

## How to Handle Warranty Disputes in the Field

1. Be calm and avoid promising coverage. "Let me check with dispatch" is
   always a safe answer.
2. Document the customer's complaint in writing with photos.
3. Quote the section of this policy that applies, but do not refuse work
   — finish the diagnostic visit and let the office handle the dispute.
4. If the customer is hostile or threatening, withdraw from the site
   safely and notify dispatch. Personal safety always overrides the
   warranty conversation.

## Edge Cases

- **Customer says "the last tech said this was covered for life."** Get
  the prior ticket number from the customer. Verify in the system before
  agreeing.
- **Customer is the third owner of the home.** Most manufacturer
  warranties are non-transferable beyond the original buyer. FieldDesk
  workmanship warranty IS transferable.
- **Original installation was by another company.** FieldDesk labor
  warranty does not apply — only the manufacturer warranty. The customer
  must contact the original installer for labor coverage.
